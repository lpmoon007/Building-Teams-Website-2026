What it is

Housewarming, in a nutshell.

Most corporate team building is either forgettable or feels like work — and most charity builds split a group into competing teams and hand a shelter a bare wooden box at the end. Housewarming does the opposite. The whole room works together toward one shared goal, everyone builds the pieces of a real home for an animal who doesn’t have one yet, and the animals themselves show up before the night is over.

Together, not against each other

No competing teams, no trophies, no judging. The room builds toward one collective goal — the difference between a contest and a night people remember.

A real home, not a bare box

Every donation leaves complete: a cozy bed, food and treats, toys and comfort items, assembled into a full welcome-home kit shelters can use right away.

The animals are in the room

When the group hits its mark, live animals arrive. It turns an activity into an experience nobody forgets.

It follows people home

Every donation is tagged. Weeks later, participants can receive a photo of the animal who ended up with what they built.

How it works

From strangers to one team — in 60 to 90 minutes.

Woven right into your reception, your group goes from finding their table to furnishing real homes for animals in need. Here’s how the night runs.

1

Arrive & team up

Guests find their team, drink in hand. The host frames the shared goal: together, the room is preparing homes for animals waiting to be adopted.

2

Build the welcome-home kits

Teams assemble and decorate cozy beds, enrichment feeders, toys and treat kits. No power tools, no mess — everyone has something to do, whether they love to build or would rather decorate.

3

The animals arrive

The room hits its goal and live animals enter as the reward. This is the emotional high point — the moment nobody expects and everybody remembers.

4

Handoff & follow-up

The finished donations are presented to the shelter partner, and guests learn how to receive a photo of the animal who gets what they built.

Housewarming FAQs

How many people can participate in Housewarming? +

From 20 to over 1,000, in teams of about 10. The program scales cleanly from an intimate offsite to a full conference general session.

How long does it take? +

60 to 90 minutes. It works woven into a reception or as a standalone activity, and we tailor the length to your agenda.

Do we need power tools or a special space? +

No. The base program uses no power tools and makes no mess, and runs in a standard ballroom or event space. You provide the room, tables and any AV; we bring everything else.

Where do the donations go? +

Everything your team builds is donated to an established local animal shelter, chosen with you. Building Teams handles the coordination and the handoff, and every donation leaves complete and ready to use.

Are live animals really there? +

Yes. Live animals arrive through vetted local partners, confirmed at booking. When the room hits its shared goal, the animals enter — it’s the emotional high point of the event.

Can we add dog houses? +

Yes. The Build add-on brings team-assembled, pre-cut dog houses as a centerpiece. They go together by hand and get decorated — no power tools, no noise. It’s priced as an addition to the base program.

Can we brand it to our company? +

Yes. Apparel, branded welcome-home kits, matching gifts and more can make the night unmistakably yours.

How much does it cost? +

Pricing is set per participant with the charitable donation included, and depends on your group size, city and whether you add the dog-house build. Tell us about your event and we’ll send a tailored proposal, usually within a business day.
