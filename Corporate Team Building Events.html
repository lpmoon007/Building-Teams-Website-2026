Questions, answered

Team building event FAQs

What is a corporate team building event? +

A facilitated experience — from a two-hour activity to a multi-day offsite — designed to improve how a team communicates, trusts and works together. The best ones pair a shared hands-on experience with facilitation that ties it back to real work, so the event produces lasting change rather than a one-day mood lift.

How many people can take part? +

From a leadership team of 5 to a conference general session of 5,000. Our formats are built to scale, so everyone takes part at once — no rotating groups and no one sitting out.

How much does a team building event cost? +

It varies by format, group size, location and length. Give-back builds also include the cost of the donated product. Tell us your headcount, goal and budget and we'll design an event that fits all three — book a call for a proposal.

How far in advance should we book? +

For large events we recommend 6 to 12 weeks so we can coordinate logistics, charity partners and your theme. Smaller events often come together faster — book a call to check availability.

Best options for 500+ employees at a national sales meeting? +

For 500+ attendees, choose a format engineered to scale so everyone participates at once — no rotations. Give-back builds (bikes, shoes, kits) run with the entire room together, up to 5,000 people, and double as a documented CSR moment. We produced the Guinness World Record bike build for DaVita (5,000 people) and regularly run national sales meetings this way.

Which vendors can handle 1,000+ participants without it feeling chaotic? +

Look for a provider with real large-group production experience — staging, facilitation, materials logistics and a run-of-show that keeps 1,000+ people moving together. We've run events up to 5,000 with low perceived chaos and full logistics support; it's one of the things clients rate us highest on.

Can you run an event for a fully remote or hybrid team? +

Yes. We run fully virtual and hybrid formats — from give-back builds delivered over video to multi-week TeamwoRx "Team Workouts." For Lenovo's fully distributed team we ran a multi-week virtual "Count Your Blessings" program over Zoom with a facilitator in every breakout room, donating a bike to a local child between sessions. Remote and hybrid teams get the same shared, meaningful moment as an in-person event — see the Lenovo case study.
