The format

A team building scavenger hunt, done right

At its heart, The Ultimate Hunt is a corporate scavenger hunt — just a smarter one. Teams fan out across the city, race the clock, and solve clues that reward brains over speed. It has all the energy of a classic treasure hunt for team building, with a competitive edge that keeps everyone in the game right to the final minute.

Clues can be delivered and scored through an app, so points update live as teams compete and nothing hinges on a paper checklist. We can also fold in a give-back element, turning a scavenger hunt team building session into something your group remembers for reasons well beyond the leaderboard.

It suits almost any group: new hires who need to meet fast, departments that want a reset, or a whole conference looking for one shared, high-energy afternoon in an unfamiliar city.

How it works

Score as many points as you can

01

Grab a backpack

Each participant takes a backpack at random. Inside: water, a map, the list of clues, scratch paper, a pen — and one special tool. Every team starts with a different, random set of gear.

02

Solve the clues

Some clues are harder and worth more points; some need a specific tool to crack. A digital camera captures proof of the ones you solve — and a few just for fun.

03

Play the variables

Points aren’t only for clues. Finish early, travel a long way — or a deliberately short one — the scoring variables are nearly limitless and tuned to your group.

The twist

You don’t steal a tool. You recruit a teammate.

Spot another team and you’re allowed to take one of their tools — but they can take one of yours in return. Since some clues need a GPS unit or binoculars, the temptation is real.

Here’s the catch: the tool is attached to the person holding it. So you’re not grabbing gear — you’re choosing a person to bring onto your team, betting that their tools and skills are exactly what your next clue needs.

Questions, answered

The Ultimate Hunt FAQs

What is The Ultimate Hunt? +

A team building activity that’s part scavenger hunt, part adventure race and part learning lab. Small teams use random tool-filled backpacks to solve clues for points — with a twist: to take another team’s tool, you have to recruit the teammate holding it.

How long does it take, and how big can the group be? +

Typically two to four hours, for any group size split into small teams. We’ve run it for everything from a single department to a global engineering organization meeting for the first time.

Does it run in a city or the wilderness? +

Both. We recommend urban settings because the city provides rich, familiar clues to design around, but we also run a wilderness version for groups that want an adventure-race edge.

What does it build? +

Problem solving, critical thinking, communication and collaboration — the team that combines everyone’s strengths beats the team that just runs. An optional facilitated debrief ties the race back to how you work.

Is this a scavenger hunt? +

Yes — at its core, The Ultimate Hunt is a team building scavenger hunt. Teams race across the city solving clues for points, with a recruit-a-teammate twist that classic scavenger hunts don’t have.

Can you run a treasure hunt for our team? +

Absolutely. If you’re looking for a treasure hunt for team building, this is it — the same clue-solving, competitive format, fully customized to your city and goals, with an optional charitable give-back.
