Where does your team really stand?

Most leaders assume their top team is more aligned than it is. This five-minute check gives you an honest read across the five things that make or break a leadership team — and the one place to start.

The check

Rate each statement 1 to 5

Answer as your team actually behaves, not as you wish it did. Be honest — the value is in the low scores. Circle a number for each.

What your overall score means

60–75

Strong

A healthy, high-trust team. Protect it — this is fragile under growth, reorgs and new members. Use offsites to stress-test strategy, not to repair.

45–59

Emerging

The foundation is there but inconsistent — usually strong on one or two dimensions and thin on the rest. Targeted work on your lowest dimension pays off fast.

Below 45

At risk

Real friction is costing you speed and talent. This rarely fixes itself with a strategy day — it needs deliberate work on trust and how the team operates.

Your first move, by weakest dimension

Trust → Start with vulnerability at the top: the most senior person goes first in admitting a miss.

Conflict → Name your team's "hallway conversations" and pull one into the room this week.

Alignment → Have each person write the top 3 priorities privately, then compare. The gaps are the work.

Accountability → Agree one shared metric the whole team owns together, not by function.

Results/ego → Change what you celebrate: reward the collective win louder than the individual one.

Now do it with the whole team

The real insight is in the gaps between your scores.
