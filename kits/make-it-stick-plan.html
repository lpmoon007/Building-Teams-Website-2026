Move 1Before the energy fades

Name the one behavior

Change sticks when it's specific and singular. Don't try to change five things. Pick the one behavior the event pointed at, and make it concrete.

The one thing we want more of after this:e.g. "ask for help sooner," "disagree in the room, not after," "share context across teams."

Move 2Within 5 business days

Run the 20-minute debrief

A short, structured conversation while it's fresh. Four questions — go around the room, don't let it become a status update.

What stuck with you from the event, a week on?
Where have you already seen the behavior — or missed a chance to?
What's one thing we should keep doing?
Who will do what, by when? (write it down)
Facilitator tip: the leader answers last, not first. If the boss frames the "right" answer up front, everyone else just agrees and the honesty is gone.
Move 3Small, spaced, deliberate

The 30-day reinforcement cadence

Behavior change needs spaced repetition, not one big push. These touch points take minutes — the point is that they happen at all.

WhenThe moveOwner & notes

Review the measurement worksheet — what changed?
Why spacing works: people forget most of a one-off experience within a week. Four small reminders over a month beats one long follow-up session — this is the same principle behind why our programs run in touch points, not a single day.
