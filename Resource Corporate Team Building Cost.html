How Much Does Corporate Team Building Cost?

Pricing5 min readBy James CarterUpdated June 2026
Quick answer

Corporate team building is almost always priced per participant, not as a flat fee. For philanthropic build events — like bike, skateboard or shoe builds — the per-person price typically includes facilitation, materials and the charitable donation itself. Total cost is driven by four things: group size, the activity, your location, and how much production the venue requires.

What’s included in the price

A complete program usually bundles the experience and the giving into one per-person rate: professional facilitation, all materials and equipment, the donated goods (the bikes, boards or shoes themselves), and on-site staff. Because the donation is built in, you’re funding both a team event and a community contribution at the same time.

The four things that move the price

Group size — larger groups lower the per-person cost but raise the total. Activity — a bike build costs more per person than a lighter activity because the donated item costs more. Location — travel, venue and local logistics vary by city. Production level — a 5,000-person general session needs more staging, AV and crew than a 30-person breakout.

How to budget for it

Start with your headcount and the impact you want to make, then ask for a per-participant quote that itemizes the donation. Most providers, including Building Teams, price per person with the charitable donation included and will tailor a proposal to your group size, city and activity. The simplest path to an accurate number is to share those three details and request a proposal.

Is it worth it?

Teams consistently rate purpose-driven events highly because the giving makes them memorable — our programs average 4.76/5 across participants. The return shows up as stronger connection, real behavioral change, and CSR impact you can report, all from a single afternoon.

Frequently asked questions

Is corporate team building priced per person or per event? +

Almost always per person. For build events the per-participant rate typically includes facilitation, materials and the charitable donation. Larger groups lower the per-person cost.

Does the price include the donated bikes or shoes? +

With Building Teams, yes — the charitable donation is included in the per-participant price, so the goods your team builds and donates are part of the program cost.

How do I get an exact quote? +

Share your group size, city and preferred activity and request a proposal. Pricing depends on those three factors plus the production your venue requires.
