Sales kickoff FAQs

What is a sales kickoff (SKO)? +

A sales kickoff, or SKO, is an annual meeting that brings a company's sales organization together to launch the new fiscal year — aligning everyone on strategy, targets and messaging, training on new products, and motivating the team for the year ahead. Most run one to three days and mix general sessions, breakouts, awards and a team-building or social moment.

What makes a good SKO team building activity? +

The best one does three things at once: energizes the room so reps leave fired up, reinforces the year's theme (not a random game), and creates one memorable peak moment they carry back to the field. A give-back build delivers all three — the whole room works toward a shared goal, and the emotional reveal makes it unforgettable.

How many people can take part? +

From a 50-person regional team to a 5,000-rep national kickoff. Our builds are engineered to scale, so the entire room participates at the same time — no rotations and no one sitting out.

Where does it fit in the agenda? +

Three slots: a high-energy opener for a cold room, a mid-agenda reset when energy dips, or — most powerfully — the closing capstone that sends reps home on a high. Most companies use it to close the general session.

How far in advance should we book? +

For large national SKOs we recommend 6 to 12 weeks so we can coordinate logistics, charity partners and your kickoff theme. Faster turnarounds are often possible — book a call to check availability.

Have you run sales kickoffs before? +

Yes — dozens of them, from regional sales teams to general sessions of thousands, for more than 1,500 companies since 2003, with a 4.76/5 participant rating.
