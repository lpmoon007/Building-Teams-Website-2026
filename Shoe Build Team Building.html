What it is

Shoes That Matter, in a nutshell.

Sole Purpose — Shoes That Matter — pairs an eye-opening look at what it means to go without with the hands-on work of building real, durable shoes for children who need them.

Perspective

Ever walk a mile in someone else’s shoes? What if they had none at all? A grounded, human reminder of what teams can do together.

Process & flow

Teams build and refine a real production line — operations in action.

Shared purpose

Everyone contributes to a tangible, countable result.

Works at any size

From 10 to 5,000, scaled to your room.

How it works

Three moves from bag-of-parts to unforgettable.

1

See the need

A short, honest framing helps the team understand what owning a first pair of shoes means to a child.

2

Build the shoes

Teams organize into a production line and assemble durable, ready-to-wear shoes at scale.

3

Create a Shoe Bank

The finished shoes form a lasting ‘Shoe Bank’ distributed to children through local partners.

The reason it sticks

Your team builds something real — for a child who needs it.

Every pair is donated to a child who has never owned shoes, through local charity partners — measurable impact your CSR team can report.

Shoes That Matter FAQs

What is the Sole Purpose activity? +

Sole Purpose (Shoes That Matter) is a shoe build team building activity. Teams assemble durable shoes for children who have never owned a pair and create a lasting Shoe Bank distributed through local partners.

Can this support CSR reporting? +

Yes. We provide participation numbers and donation totals so your team development doubles as documented community impact.

How many people can take part? +

From 10 to 5,000, including conference general sessions.

How long does it take? +

Most sessions run 60 to 90 minutes, tailored to your agenda.
