A local roundup

Top team building activities in Boston

Boston is one of the best cities in the country for getting a team out of the office — walkable, historic and packed with venues from the Seaport to Cambridge. Here are the activities we recommend most, starting with the give-back builds we run ourselves and moving into the wider city. We can build a custom program around any of them.

  • Building a Dream (bike build) — our signature experience. Teams assemble real bikes from a bag of parts, then donate them to children across the Boston area. Ideal for 5 to 5,000 people at the Boston Convention & Exhibition Center or any Seaport ballroom.
  • Get On Board (skateboard build) — a fast, high-energy build about embracing change. Every board is donated to a Boston-area kid.
  • Sole Purpose (shoe build) — teams build durable shoes for children who’ve never owned a pair, creating a lasting ‘Shoe Bank’ in the community.
  • Facilitated challenges & general sessions — our facilitators can tie a build or a fast-paced challenge into a keynote or conference general session, on-site anywhere in Greater Boston.
  • The Ultimate Hunt — a facilitated urban scavenger hunt through the Seaport and along the Freedom Trail, blending Boston history with team problem-solving.
  • Free DIY activities — short exercises your team can run in-house from our free team building activities library.

The city itself also offers plenty beyond what we operate directly, and we’re happy to build a custom program around any of them:

  • Freedom Trail scavenger hunts — self-guided or app-based hunts across downtown’s historic sites.
  • Seaport & Boston Harbor cruises — sunset and dinner sails out of the Seaport, great for larger receptions.
  • North End cooking classes — hands-on pasta and Italian cooking in Boston’s historic North End.
  • Fenway-area outings — ballpark tours, group games and the bars and eateries around Kenmore Square.
  • Escape rooms — timed puzzle rooms across Back Bay, Downtown and Cambridge for smaller breakout groups.
  • Boston Common & Public Garden challenges — outdoor field-day style games and photo challenges in the heart of the city.
  • Cambridge & Kendall Square socials — brewery tours, lawn games and campus-adjacent gatherings across the river.

Want the version that leaves the biggest mark? Pair any outing with one of our give-back experiences so your team walks away having built something for Boston’s kids.
