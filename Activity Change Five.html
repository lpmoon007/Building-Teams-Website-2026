Change Five

Change Five is a low-energy team building activity for groups of 8+ people.

Group size8+ people
EnergyLow
Time45–60 min (incl. debrief)
SettingIndoors or outdoors
Best for
ChangeProblem SolvingCompetition vs Collaboration

What it is

Change Five is a great activity for any situation in which change is necessary, which is nearly all the time. It helps participants experience the resistance to change and the pull to snap back to previous methods as quickly as possible. While most of us say change is good, as human beings we resist it until we choose to embrace it. On the surface the activity feels a little silly and simply fun, but the learning underneath it can be instrumental in embracing necessary change.

What to watch for

  • to Watch For: How quickly they change back to their original clothes after the activity is over, even though you did not tell them to chance back.
  • Be sure to look at the additional pages for more ideas on good questions to ask.
  • Have this material in front of you 6 Change Five when you are facilitating the debrief.
  • What did you notice in the group?
  • Did you look around at other people?
  • Did you think you had to take five things off or did you add anything to your outfit?

Variations

  • Keep changing the same number of things about yourself as each group does so they can see what you look like at the end.
  • Of course, you are going to look funny, which will give them 'permission' to stretch their Fimage (read about this at the end).  You can have them walk around and point out things their teammates have changed at the end.

Questions to spark the debrief

The activity is the vehicle; the debrief is where the lesson lands. Questions we ask:

  • Did the changes get easier or harder?
  • Do you see this mentality or reality influencing other aspects of your life?
  • How do these realities change or influence our lives?
  • What can you do differently to be less resisting of change?.
  • At the end of the activity and before the questions, pretend to end the activity, "Great job everyone, go ahead and take your seat again." At this point, they will put everything back to the way it was before they began the activity.

Facilitator tips

  • Why not keep your outfit like they are after the 15 changes?
  • This is because we naturally tend to gravitate toward what is comfortable.
  • This is exactly what we do when faced with change.
  • We gravitate toward what we already know.
  • At this point, you can keep your clothes with the 15 changes and continue to stand in front of the group.
