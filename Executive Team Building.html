Choosing the right approach

Offsite retreat, leadership simulation, or team coaching?

Three ways we develop leadership teams — they solve different problems, and they work best together. Here's how to tell which one you need first.

ApproachChoose it when…FormatWhat you get
Executive offsite / retreat The team needs to align and reconnect — get the real issues on the table and rebuild trust and candor at the top. 1–3 day facilitated retreat with a shared hands-on experience. Alignment, honest conversation, a shared memory, and a plan the team owns.
Team LFS (leadership simulation) You need to see how leaders actually behave under pressure — not how they describe themselves — and want a diagnostic before you invest further. Live, AI-driven crisis simulation built from your organization; leaders play themselves. A team behavioral report: evidence of how the group decides, communicates and handles conflict — coaching intelligence, not a feel-good afternoon.
Team coaching You want sustained change over months, not a single event — to embed new behaviors after a retreat or simulation surfaces them. Ongoing engagement: recurring sessions and touch points over a quarter or more. Lasting behavior change, accountability, and follow-through between the big moments.

Most senior teams start with a retreat or a Team LFS simulation to surface what's real, then use coaching to make it stick. Not sure which fits? Questions, answered

Executive team building FAQs

What is executive team building? +

Facilitated work that strengthens trust, candor and alignment among senior leaders. Unlike general team building, it confronts the real dynamics at the top — ego, competing priorities and unspoken tension — in a setting designed for honest conversation.

How is a leadership retreat structured? +

Most run one to three days and blend facilitated sessions with a shared hands-on experience. Formats range from immersive off-road retreats to private cabin and cruise settings, all tailored to your team’s goals.

How small a group do you work with? +

We work with leadership teams as small as five. Executive engagements are intentionally intimate so every voice is in the room.

Do you measure results? +

Yes. We provide light monthly touch points and data-backed follow-up so retreat breakthroughs turn into lasting behavioral change.

Should we do an offsite retreat, a leadership simulation, or team coaching? +

They solve different problems. Choose an offsite retreat when the team needs to align and rebuild trust; choose a Team LFS leadership simulation when you need to see how leaders actually behave under pressure and want a diagnostic; choose team coaching when you want sustained change over months. Most senior teams start with a retreat or simulation to surface what's real, then use coaching to make it stick.

What's the difference between executive team building and a leadership simulation? +

Executive team building is a facilitated experience that builds trust, candor and alignment. A leadership simulation is a diagnostic: it drops the team into a live crisis and captures how they actually decide, communicate and handle conflict under pressure. The retreat changes how the team works together; the simulation shows you — with evidence — exactly what to work on.
