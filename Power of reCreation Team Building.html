What it is

Power of reCreation, in a nutshell.

Somewhere along the way, teams forget how freely they once created. Power of reCreation brings that instinct back: teams get a bag of what looks like trash and race to build something remarkable — and a working soccer ball — from it. Then comes the reveal: that “trash” is exactly what children in the developing world have, and what they build their own soccer balls from. The energy your team just found gets channeled into real, durable balls for kids who need them.

Creative confidence

People rediscover they can innovate — and that it's fun.

Build-on-ideas culture

Teams practice “yes, and” instead of shooting ideas down.

Energy & play

A fast, joyful reset that re-energizes any group.

Works at any size

From 10 to 5,000, scaled to your room.

How it works

Three moves from stuck to inspired.

1

Create from “nothing”

Each team gets a bag of trash and 45 minutes to build the most creative product they can — and a soccer ball that gets tested for quality against the room.

2

The reveal

Teams learn the trash is what children in the developing world actually have — and that they build their own soccer balls from it. Perspective lands hard.

3

Give it forward

Teams decorate durable balls that are donated to kids — and every participant represents a few square feet of a new soccer field built next to a school, because a field makes children want to come and learn.

The reason it sticks

Your team builds something real — for kids who need it.

The creativity produces soccer balls donated to youth in your community — turning a burst of innovation into a gift kids will actually use, and a memory your team keeps.

Questions, answered

Power of reCreation FAQs

What is the Power of reCreation activity? +

Power of reCreation is a creativity and innovation team building activity. Teams build a creative product and a working soccer ball from a bag of trash, then learn that’s exactly what children in the developing world build from. The durable balls are donated to youth in need.

What will my team take away? +

Renewed creative confidence and a habit of building on each other's ideas — plus the energy and goodwill of giving the soccer balls to kids.

How many people can take part? +

From 10 to 5,000, including conference general sessions.

How long does it take? +

Most sessions run 60 to 90 minutes, tailored to your agenda.

What’s included? +

Everything to run it end to end: a lead facilitator and event crew, all build materials and kits, the charitable donation of what your team builds, plus full setup and teardown. You bring the room and your people; we bring the rest.

How much does it cost? +

Pricing depends on your group size and city, and it’s set per participant with the charitable donation included. Tell us your group and we’ll send a tailored proposal, usually within a business day.
